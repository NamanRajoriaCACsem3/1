Temperature Trends,
Extreme Weather Events



Climate change is driving unprecedented shifts in global temperatures, leading to more frequent and severe extreme weather events. From record-breaking heatwaves to devastating hurricanes, communities around the world are feeling the impacts of a changing climate.

4.2 Billion Dollars

Of economic loss suffered by india in 2022 due to extreme weather events and climatic disasters, most of which were caused by flood, according to a new World Meteorological Organisation (WMO) report that also flagged lightning and thunderstorms as one of the major killers in the country.


Rising temperatures are not only affecting weather patterns but also disrupting ecosystems, agriculture, and public health. Heatwaves can exacerbate air pollution and strain infrastructure, while droughts can lead to water shortages and food insecurity.

Understanding these trends is crucial for adaptation and mitigation efforts. By studying historical temperature data and monitoring current weather patterns, scientists can better predict future climate impacts and develop strategies to protect vulnerable communities.
