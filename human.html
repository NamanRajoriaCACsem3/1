Human Impact


Climate change is not just an environmental issue; it's also a human rights issue. Marginalized communities, including low-income populations, indigenous peoples, and people of color, are disproportionately affected by the impacts of climate change.






135 Million people

Could be pushed into poverty by 2030 because of climate change.

In response to these challenges, individuals, communities, and governments worldwide are mobilizing to address climate change through activism, policy changes, and sustainable practices. From youth-led climate strikes to international agreements like the Paris Agreement, there is growing momentum for climate action at all levels.

However, there is still much work to be done. Climate change requires systemic solutions that address the root causes of environmental degradation and social injustice. By centering equity and justice in climate policies and decision-making processes, we can build a more resilient and equitable future for all.

Join us in the fight against climate change. Together, we can amplify the voices of marginalized communities, advocate for climate justice, and create a world where everyone can thrive in harmony with nature.
